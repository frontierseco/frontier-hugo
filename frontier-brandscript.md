# Frontier Structural Engineering — StoryBrand BrandScript

---

## THE CHARACTER (Hero)
*Who is the customer, and what do they want?*

**Primary Audience:** Architects, general contractors, project managers, and building owners in Colorado who are managing complex construction projects.

**The One Thing They Want:**
> A structural engineering partner who shows up, communicates, and gets it right — so their project finishes on spec, on time, and without structural surprises.

**Story Gap (desire that keeps them scrolling):**
They want to be the professional who delivers. They want their client to be happy, their building to stand, and their reputation to remain intact. Structural engineering is the piece they can't control — unless they choose the right partner.

---

## HAS A PROBLEM

**Villain:** The absent, uncommunicative, or unreliable structural engineer — the one who disappears after issuing drawings, misses RFI deadlines, and isn't there when something goes sideways in the field.

**External Problem** (what they need):
> I need structural engineering services — design, drawings, and construction support — for my building project.

**Internal Problem** (what they're afraid of / feel):
> I'm worried the SE won't communicate. I'm not sure the design will match what can actually be built. And if something goes wrong mid-construction, I don't know if they'll be there to fix it.

**Philosophical Problem** (what's at stake for the world):
> Good engineers shouldn't be hard to reach. When an engineer is truly invested in a project — not just the drawings — buildings are safer and clients are better served. *Engineers ought to be accountable through closeout, not just through permit.*

---

## WHO GIVES THEM A PLAN

**Process Plan Title:** *The Frontier Project Process*

1. **Contact us with your project** — Share scope, timeline, and team. We'll tell you quickly if it's a fit.
2. **We design and document** — Structural design, engineering calculations, and stamped drawings ready for permit and construction.
3. **We see it through** — Construction administration: submittals reviewed, RFIs answered, site visits made. We stay engaged until you close out.

**Agreement Plan** (reduce fear, build trust — can live on a dedicated "How We Work" page or be implied through copy):
- We respond to RFIs within [X] business days.
- We show up for site visits when the work matters.
- We don't disappear after permit.
- We are licensed in Colorado and carry E&O insurance.
- We treat your project like our reputation depends on it — because it does.

## THAT HELPS THEM AVOID FAILURE

*(Use sparingly — these are the stakes. Mention in body copy, not in giant headlines.)*

- Structural surprises mid-construction that blow the budget and schedule
- An engineer who's hard to reach when you need a field answer
- Drawings that don't reflect constructability — designs that look good on paper but create problems in the field
- Projects that stall at the city because submittals weren't coordinated properly
- Carrying the liability when the structural piece wasn't handled right

**One-sentence fear statement** (use subtly in copy):
> "Structural problems found in the field cost two to five times what they would have cost to solve on paper."
