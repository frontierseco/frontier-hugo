## Structural Engineering Rooted in Colorado

Frontier Structural Engineering was founded in Colorado Springs and has spent two decades shaping the built environment along the Front Range and across the state. From high-alpine recreational facilities to dense urban mixed-use projects, our engineers understand the demands Colorado places on its structures: heavy snow loads, expansive soils, seismic considerations, and wind exposure that varies dramatically from the plains to the mountains.

We are licensed in Colorado and maintain active awareness of IBC amendments adopted by local jurisdictions and the geotechnical conditions specific to Colorado Springs, Denver, Pueblo, and mountain resort regions.

### Front Range Commercial and Residential Work

Our core practice covers the Front Range corridor. We have designed or reviewed structural systems for commercial office buildings, retail centers, schools, churches, medical clinics, multi-family residential buildings, and single-family custom homes. We understand the contractor and subcontractor landscape, the permitting expectations of El Paso County and the City of Colorado Springs, and the structural detailing that local inspectors look for.

### Mountain and Resort Projects

High-altitude construction brings its own set of engineering challenges: extreme ground snow loads, permafrost-adjacent soils, access constraints that affect material choices, and the need for robust connections that can be inspected and tightened after settlement. Frontier has designed structural systems for mountain cabins, trailhead shelters, and recreational amenities where durability and maintainability are as important as first cost.

### Expansive Soils and Foundation Design

Colorado's expansive clay soils are responsible for more homeowner damage than any other natural hazard in the state. Frontier engineers understand how to evaluate geotechnical reports and translate soil conditions into foundation systems that protect clients from long-term movement. We design drilled pier foundations, grade beams, mat slabs, and post-tensioned slabs appropriate for Colorado's challenging soil profile.
